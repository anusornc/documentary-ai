## Thesis
Computers did not start as digital electronic screens; they began as massive mechanical calculators of gears and steam, scaling down through electricity and silicon to fit in our pockets.

## Key facts (with sources)
- **1837 Analytical Engine**: Charles Babbage designs the first general-purpose mechanical computer, driven by steam and mechanical gears — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analytical_Engine
- **First Programmer (Ada Lovelace)**: Ada Lovelace translates Menabrea's article on the Engine and adds notes containing the first computer algorithm — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ada_Lovelace
- **1945 ENIAC**: The first programmable, electronic, general-purpose digital computer, weighing 27 tons and using over 18,000 vacuum tubes — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ENIAC
- **1947 Transistor**: John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley invent the transistor at Bell Labs, replacing fragile vacuum tubes — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transistor
- **1958 Microchip**: Jack Kilby of Texas Instruments and Robert Noyce of Fairchild Semiconductor independently invent the integrated circuit (microchip) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit
- **Personal Computer Era**: The launch of the Apple II (1977) and IBM PC (1981) popularizes computing in households — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer
- **1984 Macintosh**: Apple launches the Macintosh, popularizing the Graphical User Interface (GUI) and mouse to general consumers — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macintosh
- **1989-1991 World Wide Web**: Tim Berners-Lee invents the World Wide Web at CERN, defining HTML, HTTP, and URLs — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Wide_Web
- **2007 iPhone**: Steve Jobs unveils the iPhone, merging computing, internet, and phone into one multi-touch hand device — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPhone
- **2006 AWS & Cloud**: Amazon launches AWS (EC2/S3), pioneering modern utility cloud computing — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services
- **2010s-2020s GPU & AI**: Parallel GPU compute powers modern deep learning neural networks — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit
