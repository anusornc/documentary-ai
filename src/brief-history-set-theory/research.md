## Thesis
Set theory evolved from Cantor's radical ideas of infinite levels of infinity into a rigorous axiomatic system (ZFC) after Russell's paradox nearly destroyed the logical foundations of all mathematics.

## Key facts (with sources)
- **1874 Cantor's Birth of Set Theory**: Georg Cantor publishes his paper "On a Property of the Collection of All Real Algebraic Numbers", proving that the real numbers are uncountably infinite, establishing the first rigorous mathematics of infinity — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georg_Cantor
- **Cantor's Diagonal Argument**: Introduced in 1891, Cantor's diagonal argument is an elegant mathematical proof showing that the set of real numbers has a strictly larger cardinality than the set of natural numbers — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantor%27s_diagonal_argument
- **1901 Russell's Paradox**: Bertrand Russell discovers a contradiction in Gottlob Frege's system where the set of all sets that do not contain themselves leads to a logical contradiction, shattering the naive formulation of set theory — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russell%27s_paradox
- **Frege's Devastation**: Gottlob Frege was completing the second volume of his major work *Grundgesetze der Arithmetik* when Russell sent him the paradox. Frege added a last-minute appendix acknowledging that the foundations of his arithmetic had collapsed — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gottlob_Frege
- **Zermelo-Fraenkel Axioms (ZFC)**: Formulated by Ernst Zermelo in 1908 and refined by Abraham Fraenkel, ZFC resolves Russell's paradox by replacing naive set construction with strict axioms (such as preventing self-containing sets) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zermelo%E2%80%93Fraenkel_set_theory
- **1931 Gödel's Incompleteness**: Kurt Gödel proves that any consistent axiomatic system capable of doing basic arithmetic can never be complete (cannot prove all truths) or prove its own consistency, ending the dream of a perfect mathematical system — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6del%27s_incompleteness_theorems
