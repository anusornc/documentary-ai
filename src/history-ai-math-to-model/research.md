## Thesis (one line)
AI is not a recent software miracle; it is the physical scaling of mathematical models of the brain conceived nearly 80 years ago.

## Audience
Tech-curious viewers and general scrollers who believe AI was invented in the 2020s and consists of pure modern code. They are fascinated by historical physical machinery and hidden origins.

## The one surprising insight
The first machine to "learn" did not run on lines of computer code—it was a giant room-sized electromechanical beast (the 1958 Mark I Perceptron) that adjusted its memory weights by turning actual physical gears to vary electrical resistance.

## Subject lock
exact subject: 
- Walter Pitts (1943 mathematical logician, young prodigy) & Warren McCulloch (neurophysiologist).
- Mark I Perceptron (1958 electromechanical computer built at Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, featuring a camera made of a 20×20 grid of 400 photocells, plugboards/patchboards, and racks of motorized rotary potentiometers).

## Key facts (with sources)
- **1943 Breakthrough**: Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts prove that simple artificial binary neurons connected together can compute any logical function (AND, OR, NOT) — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McCulloch-Pitts_neuron
- **1957 Learning**: Frank Rosenblatt invents the Perceptron, adding adjustable weights that allow the network to learn from inputs — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptron
- **The Physical Weights**: The 1958 Mark I Perceptron was a physical analog computer. It learned by using electric motors to physically rotate dials (potentiometers) to change electrical resistance — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptron
- **1969 AI Winter**: Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert mathematically prove that single-layer perceptrons cannot solve the simple XOR logic problem, killing neural network research funding for a decade — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perceptrons_(book)
- **1986 Backpropagation**: Geoff Hinton and colleagues popularize Backpropagation, the algorithm that allows multi-layer networks to learn, solving the XOR problem and laying the math for deep learning — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation
